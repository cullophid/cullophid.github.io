 Andreas

My name is Andreas Møller. I am from Denmark, but I have lived in London since March 2012.

I love writing software. It provides a perfect platform for both creativity and analytical problem solving. Writing good software requires you to have an analytical and mathematical mind, but also be creative and challenge conventional thinking. I find these to be the most important qualities for a software engineer, and I try to apply them in both my professional and personal life.

This is in part where my fascination with the web comes from. The web as an application platform is growing at an incredible rate. New technologies and paradigms are being created every day, and the level of creativity we find on the web far surpasses that of any other platform. The modern web developer needs to be able to adapt, by acquiring new skills, and adapting new paradigms.

Most noticeably I experienced this when I worked for Inxite Sowftware Ltd. While working there we rewrote the entire application using Node.js and MongoDB. During this process I had to sample dozens of web frameworks and server libraries to assemble our software stack. I was also tasked with writing apps for both Android and iPhone.

In 2012 I wrote my Masters thesis on machine learning in intelligent buildings. The thesis investigated the possibilities of integrating machine learning algorithms into modern home control systems.

From 2010 to 2011 I co-founded a startup company called jollytime.dk. Though jollytime.dk never became a financial success, I acquired plenty of useful experience in web development and PHP.

You should hire me because I am passionate about web development. I spend a large part of my free time playing with new frameworks and libraries, and watching the latest Google Tech Talks. I think the web is the most amazing application platform we have ever had, and I love working with it.
